MARS

The Red Planet • Future Frontier

Back to Home

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the Red Planet because of its reddish surface, caused by iron oxide (rust) in the soil. It is the second smallest planet in our solar system, with a diameter about half that of Earth.

Mars has a thin atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide, with surface temperatures ranging from -125°C (-195°F) near the poles in winter to 20°C (70°F) at the equator at midday. The planet features the largest volcano in the solar system (Olympus Mons) and the deepest canyon (Valles Marineris).

Scientists are particularly interested in Mars because evidence suggests it had liquid water on its surface in the distant past, raising the possibility that it may have once harbored life. Today, multiple space agencies are planning crewed missions to Mars, with the goal of establishing a human presence on the planet in the coming decades.
