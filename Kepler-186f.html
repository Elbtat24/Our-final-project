Kepler-186f

Earth's Cosmic Cousin • Exoplanet Pioneer

Back to Home

Kepler-186f is an exoplanet located about 500 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Cygnus. It was discovered by NASA's Kepler Space Telescope in 2014 and holds special significance as one of the first Earth-sized planets found in the habitable zone of another star.

This fascinating world orbits a red dwarf star called Kepler-186, completing an orbit every 130 days. What makes Kepler-186f particularly exciting is its location within the "Goldilocks Zone" - where temperatures could potentially allow for the existence of liquid water on its surface, a key ingredient for life as we know it.

While scientists have not yet determined if Kepler-186f has an atmosphere or supports life, its discovery marked a major milestone in the search for habitable worlds beyond our solar system. With a radius just 10% larger than Earth's, it represents one of the most promising candidates for future study in the quest to find extraterrestrial life.
